Introduction

  • The United States Code (USC) Title 10 is the section of the United States code that outlines the role of armed forces.
    		•
  • Enacted on August 10, 1956 by the U.S. Congress
    		•
  • Includes authorities for the military branches; Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, Space Force, and Reserve components
    		•
  • This outline provides the legal basis for the roles, missions, and organization of each branch
    		•
  • See Title 10 Authorities for an example on military authorities
  • The President has the authority to convene military courts, as granted by USC Title 10.
    		•
